    What is Golf Club Planner?

    Golf Club Planner is a single-page tool that helps you choose the right club for every shot on a course you're about to play. Enter your course, bag, and scorecard — the app fetches live GPS geometry, elevation, and weather data automatically, then models each shot accounting for temperature, wind, slope, and roll.

    Everything runs in your browser with no account or install required. Your bag is saved locally between sessions.

    • Search any course in OpenStreetMap to get hole bearings, distances, and elevations
    • Live weather (forecast or historical) sets per-hole wind conditions automatically
    • Shot-by-shot table with itemized adjustments — override any club inline
    • Club usage histogram shows which clubs you'll lean on most

    Temperature Adjustment

    Ball compression and air density both change with temperature. The model uses a simple linear approximation.

    factor = 1 + (T − 70) / 1000

    Each degree above 70 °F adds 0.1 % to carry; each degree below subtracts 0.1 %.

    Example — 7-iron (150 yd) at 95 °F
    Factor:1 + (95 − 70) / 1000 = 1.025
    Carry:150 × 1.025 = 154 yds (+4 yds)
    Example — same club at 40 °F
    Factor:1 + (40 − 70) / 1000 = 0.970
    Carry:150 × 0.970 = 146 yds (−4 yds)
    Wind Adjustment

    Wind factor is multiplied on top of the temperature-adjusted carry. Headwind hurts more than tailwind helps. When GPS hole bearings are available, diagonal winds are vector-decomposed into parallel and perpendicular components so both effects apply simultaneously.

    ComponentFormula (wind speed capped at 30 mph)Max effect at 30 mph
    Headwind (parallel)1 − 0.01 × spd × |cos θ|−30%
    Tailwind (parallel)1 + 0.007 × spd × cos θ+21%
    Crosswind (perpendicular)1 − 0.0015 × spd × |sin θ|−4.5%

    θ = angle between hole bearing and wind direction. Combined factor = tailwind component × crosswind component.

    Example — 7-iron (150 yd) into 20 mph headwind at 70 °F
    Factor:1 − 0.01 × 20 = 0.80
    Carry:150 × 0.80 = 120 yds (−30 yds)
    Example — same club, 15 mph tailwind
    Factor:1 + 0.007 × 15 = 1.105
    Carry:150 × 1.105 = 166 yds (+16 yds)

    Roll is also wind-adjusted: headwind 0.5×, tailwind 1.5×, crosswind/calm 1.0×.

    Altitude Adjustment

    Thinner air at altitude reduces drag, so the ball carries farther. The model applies a linear approximation of +2% carry per 1,000 ft above sea level.

    factor = 1 + altitude_ft / 50,000

    Example — 7-iron (150 yd) at 5,280 ft (Denver)
    Factor:1 + 5280 / 50000 = 1.106
    Carry:150 × 1.106 = 166 yds (+16 yds)
    Example — same club at 2,000 ft
    Factor:1 + 2000 / 50000 = 1.040
    Carry:150 × 1.040 = 156 yds (+6 yds)

    Altitude is auto-fetched from the Open-Meteo elevation API when you select a course, and can be overridden manually in Step 5.

    Slope Adjustment

    Tee and green elevations are fetched from the Open-Meteo terrain API. A positive adjustment means the ball travels farther (downhill); negative means shorter (uphill).

    DirectionFormula
    Downhill (green lower)drop_m × 0.75 × 1.094 ≈ 0.82 yd per m
    Uphill (green higher)−rise_m × 1.094 ≈ −1.09 yd per m

    Elevation is fetched at three points per hole: the tee, a fairway landing point (at estimated drive distance along the GPS bearing), and the green. Each shot interpolates its elevation change from this profile — so a drive gets the tee-to-fairway delta and approaches get the fairway-to-green delta.

    Example — 400-yd hole, green 12 m lower
    Total adj:12 × 0.75 × 1.094 = +9.8 yds
    Drive (65%):+6.4 yds → ball flies farther
    Approach (35%):+3.4 yds → need less club
    Roll & Club Selection

    Roll is added after carry for all non-approach shots. The roll % slider sets the fraction of carry that continues rolling after landing, then wind multiplier adjusts for conditions.

    roll = carry × rollPct% × windMult

    Example — Driver (250 yd carry), 7 % roll, tailwind
    Wind mult:1.5× (tailwind)
    Roll:250 × 0.07 × 1.5 = 26 yds
    Total:250 + 26 = 276 yds

    Club selection per shot:

    • If any club can reach remaining distance → pick the one closest to the target (approach logic; no overshoot preference)
    • If no club can reach → pick the longest available (lay-up)
    • Driver is excluded from shot 2 onward
    • All adjustments (temp × wind) are factored into effective distance before selection
